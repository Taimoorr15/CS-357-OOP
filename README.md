Birthday Paradox
The birthday paradox is the counter-intuitively high probability that at least two people in a group share the same birthday. The "paradox" is that a group of only 23 people has a greater than 50% chance of a shared birthday. This happens because the number of possible pairs of people increases with the size of the group, and the probability of any pair matching is what's being calculated, not a specific date
